Introduction to TribologAI

TribologAI is a collaborative project with Marjetas Oy, a company specializing in road and infrastructure services for municipalities. The project aims to analyze weather and road data to identify factors affecting road friction and develop a Machine Learning (ML) or Neural Network (NN) algorithm to predict road conditions a few hours in advance using 10-minute interval data.
Our analysis revealed inconsistencies in variables and time periods across datasets. One team member focused on Marwis mobile sensor data, while another worked on the Tie 4 Jyväskylä dataset. Despite cleaning the data, key features influencing friction, especially around temperatures between -10°C and +5°C, were missing, limiting model accuracy.
To enhance the dataset, we incorporated open-source weather data from Jyväskylä Airport, sourced from FMI's website, and merged it with existing data using timestamps. Below, we present the analysis and plots from both datasets, individually and combined.

Correlation Analysis and Data Distribution Insights

Checked correlations between features from both datasets. Air temperature and temperature, as well as air pressure and road temperature, showed moderate positive correlations with friction. Snow displayed a strong negative correlation, while relative humidity showed a moderate negative correlation with friction. However, the lack of correlation between ice and friction was unexpected due to insufficient ice observations in the dataset.
Compared the distributions of temperature and air_temp columns and assessed the reliability of the imputation process for missing values.
Evaluated the distribution of dewpoint from Tie 4 and airport weather columns to confirm if they aligned and supported reliance on the imputation method.
Analyzed how dewpoint, relative humidity, and friction are distributed with snow. Observed that friction decreases significantly with moderate snow at temperatures between -10°C and 0°C, and further drops under high humidity conditions.

Initial Distribution of data and data cleaning

The distribution of various features in the dataset was analyzed and adjusted as follows:
The 'Water_Film_Surface_mm' initially had a non-normal distribution, but after fixing the issue, its distribution improved significantly.
Similarly, the 'Ice_Percentage' exhibited a skewed distribution, which was corrected for better balance.
The 'Amb_T' (ambient temperature) showed an almost normal distribution and was retained as is.
The 'RH_at_Road_T' lacked sufficient data, making it unsuitable for analysis, so it was removed.
The 'Road_Surface_Temperature' had an imbalanced distribution. Instead, we opted to use 'Amb_T', which showed better balance.
The 'Dew_Point_T' had insufficient data, and due to the inability to recalculate it accurately, it was also removed.
The 'Road Condition' column, calculated from Water Film, Temperature, and Ice Percentage, was deemed redundant for model training and excluded.
The 'Friction' initially displayed a normal distribution with some imbalances at higher levels.
